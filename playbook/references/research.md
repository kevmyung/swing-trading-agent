# Research Risk Interpretation

Research flags come from the research analyst agent. The flag level tells you
something was found — your job is to assess whether it matters for the
specific trade thesis.

## Risk Levels

- **none**: No material findings. Proceed with normal evaluation.
- **flag**: Something noteworthy but not disqualifying. Assess relevance below.
- **veto**: Analyst recommends against the trade. Assess severity below.

## Assessing Relevance

Not all flags carry equal weight. The key question: does this finding
threaten the specific thesis, or is it background noise?

**Material and thesis-relevant** (lean SKIP or EXIT):
- Fraud, accounting scandal, regulatory halt
- Confirmed earnings miss or guidance cut
- Direct supply chain disruption for a momentum thesis
- Fundamental deterioration (debt downgrade, dividend cut)

**Indirect or already priced in** (sizing concern, not blocker):
- Sector peer downgrade — relevant context but not ticker-specific
- Stale news already reflected in the price decline — the oversold
  condition may itself be the market's reaction to this event
- Minor litigation, analyst target change, or management commentary
  without earnings impact

**Stale or low-quality** (discount heavily):
- News older than the current price action — if the stock has already
  declined to reflect the event, the flag is confirming what price
  already shows, not adding new information
- Sector-level retrospectives or thematic coverage

## Strategy Context

- **MR entries with research flag**: The flag may explain *why* the stock
  is oversold — which is the setup you're trading. If the flag is a
  one-time event already reflected in the decline, the MR thesis
  (temporary dislocation → snap-back) can still hold. Adjust size, don't
  automatically reject.
- **MOM entries with research flag**: Check if the risk threatens the
  trend thesis. A sector headwind on a strong individual trend (high ADX,
  Stage 2) is less concerning than a company-specific issue that could
  stall the trend.
- **Existing positions with research flag**: Has something changed since
  entry? A new flag on a held position is more actionable than a flag
  that existed at entry and was already factored in.

## Veto Override

A research veto on material grounds (fraud, halt, confirmed deterioration)
is thesis-invalidating — lean strongly toward SKIP or EXIT regardless of
how attractive the quantitative setup looks. The quant signals cannot
account for information the veto captures.

A veto on soft grounds (stale coverage, sector sentiment, minor event)
adds caution but does not automatically invalidate. Assess whether the
specific concern has a plausible path to affecting the trade within your
holding period.
