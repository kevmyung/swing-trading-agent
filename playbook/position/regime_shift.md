# Regime Shift During Hold

When the market regime changes while you hold a position, this is new
information that must be addressed in your reasoning.

## Impact by Transition

- **TRENDING → TRANSITIONAL** while holding MOM: Reduce conviction, tighten
  stop. Trend tailwind is weakening but not gone.
- **TRENDING → MEAN_REVERTING** while holding MOM: Environment no longer
  supports the momentum thesis. Tighten stop and lean toward exit — unless
  the stock's own trend structure remains strong (high ADX, positive mom_z,
  Stage 2 weekly) or it shows clear relative strength vs the market.
- **MEAN_REVERTING → TRENDING** while holding MR: The MR environment that
  supported entry is shifting. For fresh MR positions (< 5 days), the
  thesis may still play out on its own timeline. For stalling MR positions
  (> 7 days, not recovering), the regime shift adds urgency to exit.
- **Any regime → HIGH_VOLATILITY**: Tighten stops on all positions.

## Key Principle

A regime shift is new information. If your HOLD reasoning does not reference
the regime change, you are ignoring it. But a regime shift alone is not an
automatic EXIT — assess the position's own health within the new context.

Fresh positions (< 5 days) deserve more patience through regime shifts
than stale positions that were already underperforming.
